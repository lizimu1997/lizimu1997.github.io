The goal of this project is to design a series of service systems that can improve and enhance the service area's user experience with China's highway service area.

In order to achieve this goal, it is first necessary to analyze China's highway service level and its changing trends. This analysis collected the mileage of highways in various regions of China, the number of service areas and the changes in the number of employees in various passenger transportation industries across the country.

Combining with the changes in the number of various types of vehicles in China, it shows the development trend of China's way service level.

Survey of users and staffs



We interviewed practitioners and service personnel in several service areas. They were asked about the problems and their expectations for the service area.The result reflects a series of deficiencies and potential development trends in the existing service areas.




“Carrying out long-distance cargo transportation across the country, and having to return to the truck to rest after eating at the high-speed service station. Put a simple bed behind the truck. Sometimes only eat instant noodles in the car to save money and time.”


— Truck driver


“Don’t have long driving experience, so always need to go to the service area to rest. If there is a quiet, hygienic and convenient rest room,hope to use it. Hope to have a comfortable and enjoyable process when go out to work or travel.”


— Car driver


“Generally work at night to manage the safety of the parking lot and its surroundings. There are frequent thefts from late night to morning, which is very hard work.”


— Security


“Convenience stores are open from 7:00 in the morning to 10:00 at night, and there is a lot of traffic on holidays and weekends. Although a driver’s lounge was set up, it was transformed into a storage room. Usually rest in his car.”
